## INTRODUCTION

Water is a fundamental component of life and a critical resource for the functioning of ecosystems and human societies. Despite technological advances and economic development, global water resources are under increasing pressure. Climate change, rising temperatures, and altered precipitation patterns are intensifying water scarcity worldwide, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions (IPCC, 2023). These changes are not only reducing water availability but are also reshaping agricultural production systems at a global scale.

Agriculture is the sector most directly dependent on water resources, with irrigation playing a key role in stabilizing crop yields under variable climatic conditions (FAO, 2017). However, growing food demand driven by population growth and changing consumption patterns is intensifying the challenge of increasing agricultural productivity while managing increasingly limited water resources more efficiently (Tilman et al., 2011; Alexandratos and Bruinsma, 2012). This challenge demands innovative approaches to water management in agricultural systems.

These challenges are particularly pronounced in the Mediterranean region, which is widely recognized as a global climate change hotspot (Giorgi, 2006; Cramer et al., 2018). Within this context, olive cultivation occupies a distinctive position. As one of the oldest and most emblematic agricultural practices in the region, olive farming dominates Mediterranean agricultural landscapes and plays a central role in rural economies, land use, and cultural heritage (International Olive Council, 2021; Loumou and Giourga, 2003).

The convergence of these agricultural challenges with rapid technological advancement presents a transformative opportunity. We are currently witnessing a global technology revolution driven by the Internet of Things (IoT)—a paradigm shift that is fundamentally reshaping industries, economies, and societies (Friha et al., 2021). In agriculture, IoT-based smart irrigation systems represent a fresh and rapidly growing market niche, where interconnected sensors, wireless communication networks, and cloud-based platforms enable real-time monitoring and automated irrigation control (García et al., 2020; Navarro et al., 2020). This revolution represents the emergence of entirely new markets and business models, with early adopters already demonstrating substantial competitive advantages through improved resource efficiency, reduced operational costs, and enhanced sustainability credentials (Ahmed et al., 2023; Tzerakis et al., 2023). However, despite this demonstrated potential, widespread adoption in Mediterranean olive cultivation remains limited, particularly among small and medium-scale producers. This represents both a significant market opportunity and a critical sustainability challenge.

This thesis addresses this gap by developing and evaluating an accessible, market-ready IoT-based irrigation system specifically designed for olive groves in Mediterranean climatic conditions. The research aims to demonstrate how emerging IoT technologies can be effectively deployed to create practical, economically viable solutions that improve irrigation efficiency, support sustainable water management, and position Mediterranean olive producers to capitalize on the connected agriculture revolution while contributing to the climate resilience of regional production systems.
