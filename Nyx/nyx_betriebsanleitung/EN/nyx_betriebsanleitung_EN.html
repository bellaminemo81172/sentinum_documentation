1. Warnings and Safety Instructions

Warnings and important information regarding potential hazards or damage.

Important notes required for the smooth operation of the device.

Please note:

  • Follow the safety instructions and installation guidelines in this manual and the mounting list.
  • Ensure that the installation environment complies with the specified operating conditions. Always observe temperature and other limits.
  • The device may only be used within the technical specifications.
  • The device may only be used for its intended purpose.
  • Safety and functionality can no longer be guaranteed if the device is modified or altered.
  • The purpose of the sensor is to monitor indoor air quality.
  • Do not use the sensor in safety‑critical applications.
  • The sensor is not suitable for monitoring carbon monoxide (CO).
  • The sensor is not a smoke detector.
  • Do not mount the sensor on ceilings or floors.
  • The device is approved for operation up to a maximum of 2000 meters above sea level.
  • A minimum distance of 20 cm between the device and persons must be maintained.

If the device is installed incorrectly:

  • It may not function properly.
  • It may become permanently damaged.
  • It may pose a risk of injury.

Handling and disposal notes:

  • Do not open the sensor.
  • Improper handling, such as mechanical stress or dropping the device, may cause damage.
  • Dispose of the device according to local regulations for electronic waste.
  • Contains a lithium battery (UN38.3 compliant) — do not burn, disassemble, or short‑circuit.
  • The secondary cell is a supercapacitor — do not damage or dispose of improperly.
  • Dispose of batteries separately according to local recycling regulations.
  • Environmentally friendly disposal via authorized collection points is recommended.
  • Do not dispose of in household waste.

Please note regarding the CO₂ sensor:

  • The CO₂ module is highly sensitive to mechanical stress. Even small drops, shocks, or impacts may cause damage and lead to incorrect measurements.

5. Installation and Mounting

If the sensor remains easily accessible after installation, mount the sensor first and activate it afterwards.

If the sensor is no longer accessible after installation, activate it first and mount it afterwards.

Mounting Method Recommended Accessories
Wall mount (screws) 2× M3 countersunk screws
Wall mount (adhesive strips) 2× adhesive strips

Please note:

  • Do not insert objects or body parts into the ventilation slots.
  • Do not mount the sensor on the ceiling or floor.
  • Do not mount the sensor above two meters.
  • Mount the sensor indoors on a wall at a height of 1.50–1.80 m.
  • Ensure the sensor receives at least 7 hours of direct sunlight at 400 lux per day.
  • Ensure the LEDs are positioned at the lower left during installation.

5.1 Mounting the Wall Bracket with Screws

Please note: Ensure the mounting surface is flat and suitable for screws to avoid damaging the housing.

Mount the wall bracket using two M3 countersunk screws as shown. The snap‑in mechanism is at the bottom and faces away from the wall.

After mounting, check the stability by gently pulling on the bracket. Tighten the screws if necessary.

Once the bracket is securely mounted, proceed to step 5.3.

5.2 Mounting the Wall Bracket with Adhesive Strips

Please note:

  • Ensure the surface is clean, dry, smooth, and adhesive‑friendly.
  • Avoid rough, porous, or damp surfaces.
  • Press the bracket firmly for several seconds after attaching it.

Attach the wall bracket using the two adhesive strips in the marked area. The snap‑in mechanism is at the bottom and faces away from the wall.

Check the stability by gently pulling on the bracket. Ensure the adhesive strips are fully attached.

Once the bracket is secure, proceed to step 5.3.

5.3 Mounting the Sensor onto the Wall Bracket

  1. Insert the sensor from above into the wall bracket until it clicks into the upper holder.
  2. Tilt the sensor backward so it snaps securely into the bracket and locking mechanism.
  3. Ensure the sensor is fully seated and firmly attached.

6.3 Intended Use of the Sensor

The solar‑powered IoT sensor with CO₂ and humidity detection is ideal for monitoring indoor air quality and environmental conditions. It provides precise data and operates autonomously using its integrated solar panel.

  • Optimization of indoor air quality: Continuous CO₂ measurement ensures a healthy indoor climate and timely ventilation.
  • Humidity monitoring: Prevents mold formation and maintains optimal humidity levels.
  • Energy‑efficient operation: Solar‑powered and ideal for areas without power outlets.
  • Customizable settings: Adjustable CO₂ and humidity thresholds for alerts and notifications.
  • Integration into smart building systems: Compatible with HVAC systems.
  • Compliance with environmental standards: Supports air quality monitoring in public buildings.
  • Remote monitoring: Uses LoRaWAN for low‑power data transmission and notifications.

8. Cleaning and Maintenance

To ensure reliable operation and long service life, the sensor should be cleaned regularly:

  • Remove dust and dirt from the solar panel regularly to ensure optimal energy absorption. Use a soft, clean microfiber cloth. For stubborn dirt, a slightly damp cloth may be used — avoid cleaning agents or abrasive materials.
  • Clean the housing and especially the ventilation slots with a dry or slightly damp microfiber cloth. Ensure that no moisture enters the device.
  • Perform cleaning regularly, especially in dusty or pollen‑rich environments, to maintain long‑term sensor functionality.
  • Regularly check the secure fit of both the sensor and the wall mount.
  • Avoid alcohol‑based or solvent‑based cleaning agents, as they may damage the sensor surface.
